## Key Principles

1. **Cats hide illness well** — Monitor for subtle changes in behaviour, appetite, and litter habits
2. **Individual needs matter** — Kittens, seniors, and cats with medical conditions need adapted care
3. **Slow introductions work best** — Whether introducing new cats or new environments, patience is key
4. **Enrichment prevents problems** — Boredom leads to unwanted behaviours; provide scratching surfaces, climbing space, and regular play
5. **Prevention over treatment** — Regular vet care, proper nutrition, and a safe home prevent many problems

## When to Contact a Veterinarian

Seek immediate veterinary care for:
- Trouble breathing or repeated panting
- Repeated vomiting or inability to keep food down
- Inability to urinate or straining in the litter box
- Collapse, seizures, or loss of consciousness
- Pale gums or severe lethargy
- Sudden severe pain

For non-urgent concerns, schedule a check-up during regular business hours. Annual wellness exams are recommended for adult cats; senior cats (10+) benefit from twice-yearly visits.
