# Cat Behavior Guide

Cat behavior is often subtle. Tail position, ear angle, posture, and vocalization all help explain how a cat is feeling.

## Slow Blinking

A slow blink is usually a relaxed social signal. Humans can slowly blink back to show calm, friendly attention.

## Kneading

Kneading is a normal comfort behavior. Cats may knead blankets, beds, or people when they feel secure.

## Scratching

Scratching marks territory, stretches muscles, and maintains claws. Redirect scratching by placing sturdy scratchers near favorite resting spots.

## Hiding

Hiding can be normal during stress, illness, or environmental change. A cat that suddenly hides more than usual should be monitored and may need veterinary care.

## Play Aggression

Young cats may bite or pounce during play. Use toys instead of hands so the cat learns appropriate targets.
