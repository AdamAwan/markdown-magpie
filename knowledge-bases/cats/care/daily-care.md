# Cat Care Basics

Cats require consistent, daily care to stay healthy, happy, and well-adjusted. This guide covers the essential routines every cat owner should maintain.

## Feeding Basics

### Food Schedule

**Adult cats** typically do well with:
- Two measured meals per day (morning and evening)
- About 12 hours apart
- Consistent feeding times help with digestion and litter box habits

**Kittens** require more frequent meals:
- 8-12 weeks: 4 meals per day
- 3-6 months: 3 meals per day
- 6-12 months: 2 meals per day
- Transition gradually to adult feeding after 1 year

**Senior cats** (10+ years):
- May benefit from 3-4 smaller meals per day
- Easier to digest, prevents overeating
- Consult your vet for specific recommendations

### Portion Control

**Measure portions:**
- Use a measuring cup or kitchen scale
- Follow guidelines on your cat food label based on weight
- Average adult cat: 200-300 calories per day (varies by cat)

**Prevent obesity:**
- Weigh your cat monthly
- Adjust portions if weight changes
- Obesity increases risk of diabetes, arthritis, and joint problems

**Monitor appetite:**
- Sudden changes in eating (more or less) warrant a vet visit
- Refusal to eat for 24+ hours is a medical concern
- Increased hunger can indicate illness (diabetes, hyperthyroidism)

### Types of Food

**Dry kibble:**
- Convenient and shelf-stable
- Choose high-quality brands meeting AAFCO standards
- Less water content — doesn't replace drinking water
- Can help with dental health slightly
- Watch for excessive thirst/urination (possible diet-related issues)

**Wet or canned food:**
- Higher moisture content (70-80% vs. 10% in kibble)
- Better for urinary and kidney health
- More appealing to many cats
- Spoils quickly after opening — use within 24-48 hours
- More expensive than dry food

**Mixed feeding:**
- Some people feed wet food at one meal, dry at another
- Provides variety and benefits of both types
- Ensure total portions stay consistent

**Semi-moist food:**
- Often higher in sugar and additives
- Not generally recommended as a primary diet
- Can be used occasionally as a treat

## Hydration

### Water Availability

**Fresh water at all times:**
- Change water bowl daily (twice daily in hot weather)
- Use a clean bowl or fountain
- Cats should always have access, especially if eating dry food

**Encourage drinking:**
- Some cats prefer running water — fountains can help
- Moving water feels fresher and more natural to cats
- Bowls should be separate from food (cats don't naturally drink near where they eat)

**Signs of adequate hydration:**
- Cat uses litter box normally (not straining, normal frequency)
- Skin is moist and elastic
- Cat seems alert and energetic

**Signs of dehydration:**
- Excessive thirst or not drinking at all
- Straining in litter box or not urinating
- Dry, flaky skin
- Lethargy or confusion
- Consult your vet immediately

### Wet Food Benefits

Wet food helps with hydration, especially important for:
- Cats prone to urinary issues
- Senior cats who don't drink enough
- Indoor cats with less active lifestyles

## Litter Box Management

### Box Setup

**Number of boxes:**
- Aim for one per cat plus one extra
- Example: 2 cats = 3 boxes
- More boxes = fewer conflicts and better monitoring of health

**Placement:**
- Quiet, accessible location away from food and water
- Ideally in more than one location in multi-level homes
- Not in high-traffic areas where the cat feels exposed
- Avoid loud appliances (washing machine, furnace)

**Type of box:**
- Open boxes are most common
- Large boxes (24"+ long) prevent overflow and spillage
- Covered boxes: some cats prefer privacy; others find them claustrophobic
- Top-entry boxes: help contain litter scatter; some cats dislike them

### Litter Type

**Common litters:**
- **Clumping clay**: Absorbent, easy to scoop, affordable (most common)
- **Non-clumping clay**: Cheaper but less convenient; requires full changes
- **Natural/eco-friendly**: Paper, corn, wheat, pellets (watch for dust)
- **Crystal litter**: Low tracking, low dust, but some cats reject it

**Avoiding issues:**
- Don't switch litter brands abruptly — transition gradually over 1-2 weeks
- Most cats are particular about litter — sudden changes cause avoidance
- Unscented litter is often preferred to heavily scented varieties
- Fine-textured litter is preferred by most cats (feels more like natural soil)

### Daily Maintenance

**Daily scooping:**
- Remove clumps and solid waste once or twice daily
- This keeps the box fresh and allows monitoring of health
- One unscooped box may cause the cat to use another location

**Full changes:**
- Replace all litter weekly (varies by litter type and box count)
- Wash box monthly with mild soap and water
- Dry completely before adding fresh litter

**Problem prevention:**
- Odour control depends on regular maintenance, not fancy litters
- Litter scatter is normal; use a mat to contain it
- Never use litter as punishment or scold the cat at the box

## Grooming & Nail Care

### Regular Brushing

**Benefits:**
- Reduces shedding and hairballs
- Strengthens bond with your cat
- Allows you to notice skin problems, lumps, or parasites early
- Many cats find brushing relaxing and pleasant

**Frequency:**
- Short-haired cats: 1-2 times per week
- Long-haired cats: 3-5 times per week (daily during shedding season)
- Some cats love brushing; others tolerate it briefly

**How to brush:**
- Use a cat-specific brush or comb
- Brush in the direction of hair growth
- Be gentle, especially around sensitive areas
- Make it short and positive

### Nail Trimming

**How often:**
- Every 2-4 weeks depending on growth
- Indoor cats' nails grow faster and need more frequent trimming
- Outdoor cats wear nails naturally

**How to trim:**
1. Get your cat comfortable and calm
2. Gently press the paw to extend the nail
3. Trim only the clear part of the nail, not the pink quick
4. If you hit the quick, it bleeds and hurts — stop and comfort your cat
5. Use cat-specific nail clippers

**If unsure:**
- Ask your vet to show you the first time
- Many vets trim nails at wellness visits
- Professional groomers can help if your cat is difficult

### Dental Care

**Why it matters:**
- Dental disease affects overall health
- It's painful and can lead to tooth loss
- Prevention is easier than treatment

**Care options:**
- Brush your cat's teeth several times per week (with cat-specific toothpaste)
- Some studies show certain dry foods help slightly with dental health
- Professional cleaning by a vet when needed

## Enrichment & Environment

### Why Enrichment Matters

Enrichment prevents:
- Boredom and depression
- Destructive behaviour
- Weight gain and obesity
- Stress-related illness

### Environmental Enrichment

**Window perches:**
- Cats love watching outside activity
- Install secure window shelves
- This provides mental stimulation without physical activity

**Climbing structures:**
- Cat trees, wall-mounted shelves, or climbing paths
- Indoor cats need vertical space
- Provides exercise, territory, and escape from perceived threats

**Hiding spots:**
- Boxes, tunnels, or covered beds
- Cats feel safer with places to retreat
- Reduce stress in multi-cat or multi-pet households

**Scratching surfaces:**
- Multiple scratchers in different areas and orientations
- Vertical (cat posts) and horizontal (scratching pads)
- Necessary for claw maintenance and marking territory
- Helps redirect scratching away from furniture

### Interactive Play

**Wand toys:**
- String and feather toys mimic prey
- 10-15 minutes of play, 2+ times daily
- Exercise and mental stimulation
- Good bonding opportunity

**Solo play:**
- Balls, mice, or other small toys
- Cats can entertain themselves
- Rotate toys to maintain interest

**Puzzle feeders:**
- Make eating more engaging
- Provide mental stimulation
- Slow eating slightly
- Some cats find them frustrating — monitor reaction

**Play sessions:**
- Morning and evening play often aligns with cat hunting instincts
- Let the cat "win" sometimes and catch the toy
- Stop if the cat seems uninterested or tired

## Daily Routine

A consistent daily routine helps cats feel secure and healthy:

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Morning | Feed, refresh water, scoop litter, interactive play |
| Midday | Fresh water, check litter |
| Evening | Feed, interactive play, quiet time together |
| Before bed | Final litter scoop, ensure fresh water |
