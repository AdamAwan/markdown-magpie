# Cat Health Notes

Cats often hide illness, so small behavior changes can matter. Appetite, litter box habits, grooming, energy, and breathing should be watched closely.

## Vet Care

Cats should have regular veterinary checkups. Vaccines, parasite prevention, dental care, and weight monitoring are common parts of preventive care.

## Warning Signs

Urgent warning signs include trouble breathing, repeated vomiting, inability to urinate, collapse, seizures, pale gums, or sudden severe pain.

## Hairballs

Occasional hairballs can happen, especially in long-haired cats. Frequent hairballs, coughing, or appetite changes should be discussed with a veterinarian.

## Weight

Obesity increases the risk of diabetes, arthritis, and other health problems. Use measured portions and play to help maintain a healthy weight.
