# Cat Diet: What Do Cats Eat?

Cats are obligate carnivores, meaning their diet must consist primarily of animal-based proteins. This document clarifies the types of food that meet their nutritional needs.

## Essential Nutrients

- **Protein**: Cats require high levels of animal protein from sources such as chicken, fish, or beef.
- **Taurine**: An essential amino acid found only in animal tissue. Deficiency can cause health problems.
- **Fats**: Provide energy and aid in absorption of fat-soluble vitamins.
- **Vitamins and Minerals**: Especially vitamin A, B vitamins, calcium, and phosphorus.

## Acceptable Food Types

### Commercial Cat Food
- **Dry kibble**: Convenient, but ensure it's high-quality and meets AAFCO standards.
- **Wet/canned food**: Higher moisture content, beneficial for urinary tract health.
- **Semi-moist food**: Often higher in sugar and less recommended.

### Raw Diets
- **Pre-made commercial raw**: Formulated to be nutritionally complete.
- **Homemade raw**: Requires careful balancing of ingredients to avoid deficiencies; consult a veterinarian.

### Home-Cooked Meals
- Must include appropriate supplements to meet feline nutritional requirements.

## Foods to Avoid
- **Toxic items**: Onions, garlic, chocolate, grapes, raisins.
- **Dairy**: Many cats are lactose intolerant.
- **Raw fish or eggs**: Risk of bacteria and thiamine deficiency.

## Summary
Always provide fresh water and a balanced diet tailored to your cat's life stage (kitten, adult, senior). For specific recommendations, consult your veterinarian.
